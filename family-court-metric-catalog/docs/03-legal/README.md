## Sequencing note

The RSMo draft (document 05) is NOT intended for introduction until month 31-36 of the pilot. The Executive Summary (docs/01-framework/00) explicitly states this. The draft exists so stakeholders can evaluate the shape of eventual statutory authorization; it is not a near-term legislative ask.

## Evidentiary shield

RSMo § 452.317 (drafted in document 05) provides an evidentiary shield preventing adversarial weaponization of individual compliance data. The shield is essential. Any tool that helps a parent document compliance could be subpoenaed and used against them without this protection.

## Interaction with coercive-control content

The Asymmetry Principle (docs/07-coercive-control/cc-01) interprets how the evidentiary shield operates in coercive-control cases. The shield is symmetric in its letter but asymmetric in its effect — it protects a targeted parent's documentation from being characterized as "uncooperative" while the controlling parent's behavior remains the subject of documentation. This is a governance-level interpretation, not a statutory change.

## Use by other jurisdictions

The MOU and DUA templates are drafted for Missouri but structured to be adaptable. State-specific revisions — suppression thresholds, ethical standards, sovereign immunity — should be handled by the adopting jurisdiction's counsel. The structural elements (audit rights, term/termination, prohibited uses) are portable.
