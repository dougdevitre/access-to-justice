# Coercive Control Mini-Binder

> ⚠️ **DRAFT FOR EXPERT REVIEW.** Every document in this directory is a technical draft prepared by a product author who is not a coercive-control clinician or a survivor advocate. None of this content is ready to ship operationally. It is shared to support the Expert Review Convening (CC-07) and preliminary stakeholder conversations.

## Why this mini-binder exists

The main OSCA Outreach Binder (the other `01-` through `06-` directories in `docs/`) establishes the Catalog's defensibility but does not adequately address coercive control or manufactured conflict. Measurement systems that handle these topics with symmetric bilateral frameworks routinely cause harm to targeted parents. This mini-binder is the substantive expansion that addresses the gap — in draft form, pending expert review.

## What you should NOT do with this material

**Do not distribute to general audiences.** A journalist, a legislator, or a family-court participant reading this material out of context could produce coverage that either weaponizes the framework or dismisses it. Both outcomes harm survivors.

**Do not integrate into the main OSCA binder.** The Expert Review Convening (CC-07) contemplates integration as a post-convening decision, deliberately.

**Do not cite CC-04 metrics as operational.** None of them have been validated. They are proposals pending expert review.

**Do not use CC-05's lethality-response technical recommendations as final product decisions.** The product author explicitly punts these decisions to MOCADSV and clinical advisors. Implement the lower-stakes recommendations only; hold high-stakes decisions for the Convening.

## What you SHOULD do with this material

**Share with MOCADSV leadership** if you are initiating conversations about potential partnership. Lead with CC-00, CC-01, and CC-07.

**Share with a clinical advisor** if you are scoping a retention for the Expert Review Convening. Lead with CC-02, CC-03, and CC-04.

**Use CC-05 internally** to guide product decisions at CoTrackPro that can be made now without waiting for the Convening — the lower-stakes items (safety-framed UI language, sparse notifications, device-local defaults).

**Treat every document as a conversation opener, not an endpoint.**

## Foundational literature

Every document in this directory draws on the published research literature. The foundational works are:

- Stark, E. (2007). *Coercive Control: How Men Entrap Women in Personal Life.* Oxford University Press.
- Katz, E. (2022). *Coercive Control in Children's and Mothers' Lives.* Oxford University Press.
- Hardesty, J. L. & colleagues (2006-2023). Various works on post-separation coercive control.
- Johnson, M. P. (2008). *A Typology of Domestic Violence.* Northeastern University Press.
- Douglas, H. (2018). Legal systems abuse and coercive control. *Criminology & Criminal Justice.*

See [CC-02](./cc-02-literature-and-instruments.docx) for the full reading list.
