## What the Adoption Roadmap adds to the binder

The first 20 documents establish defensibility — the framework is methodologically sound. Document 21 addresses a different question: why would an attorney, judge, or mediator actually click through the Catalog at 4 pm on a Tuesday?

Fifteen initiatives across four tiers:

- **Tier 1 (Months 1-9):** Case Planning Calculator, Motion Strategy Reference, Private Judicial Dashboard, Bench Card, Exhibit Generator.
- **Tier 2 (Months 4-15):** CLE Credit, Client Templates, Mediator Indicator, Amicus Capacity.
- **Tier 3 (Months 10-24):** Catalog-Informed Practice credential, Judicial Education Partnership, Settlement Conference Protocol.
- **Tier 4 (Months 18-36):** Non-Missouri Case Studies, Friend-of-Court Consults, Case Management Integration.

The Roadmap also names what no other binder document says: the most important missing piece is not technical. It is a named, respected family court judge willing to be publicly associated with the project. Relationship-building, not feature-building.

## Bench Card

The two-sided landscape PDF ([bench-card.pdf](./bench-card.pdf)) is designed to be printed and laminated. Front: IS / IS NOT panels (green/red). Back: disclosure tier reference + judicial protections + four-step onboarding.

This is the single highest-impact adoption artifact in the roadmap and the cheapest to produce. Distribute before any formal outreach.
