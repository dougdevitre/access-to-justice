# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

This project works on family court systems — a domain where real people are in real distress. Children's lives are shaped by the decisions these systems make. Survivors of intimate partner violence, including coercive control, interact with these systems under elevated personal risk.

We commit to making this repository a space where the work can proceed honestly, where survivors and their advocates are treated with the care the subject matter demands, and where bad-faith use of the repository as a litigation or advocacy weapon is prevented.

This Code of Conduct applies to everyone participating in the repository: maintainers, contributors, reviewers, issue filers, and anyone who engages with the project in any form.

## Expected behavior

- **Treat survivor-safety provisions as non-negotiable.** The Asymmetry Principle, the Screening Protocol's safeguards, the Tier D never-disclosed category — these are protective architecture. Arguments about relaxing them are treated with skepticism.
- **Engage in good faith.** Disagree with arguments, not people. Assume others are acting in good faith unless there is clear evidence otherwise.
- **Be precise with language.** The distinction between "high conflict" and "coercive control" matters. The distinction between "aggregate" and "individual" data matters. Precision protects people.
- **Respect confidentiality.** Case details, identifying information, and non-public stakeholder communications stay private.
- **Credit others' work.** Especially survivor-led scholarship and advocacy organizations whose decades of work this framework draws on.

## Behaviors that will result in removal

- **Harassment** of any participant, including via private communication initiated from repository interaction.
- **Re-identification attempts** of any party represented in aggregate data, even as thought experiment.
- **Advocacy for removing survivor-safety protections** (the 25-petition floor, the k-anonymity threshold, the Track C silent-route, the lethality-flag Tier D designation, the Methodology Board DV advocate seat's blocking authority, etc.) without clinical or advocacy-organization credentials.
- **Using repository interactions to gather evidence for active family court litigation.** This is the single fastest path to removal. The repository is not a discovery venue.
- **Publishing or sharing draft coercive-control content** outside the expert-review channels in ways that predictably reach people who could be harmed by the draft status.
- **Weaponizing the repository against specific individuals** (named judges, attorneys, parties, advocates).
- **Bad-faith methodology proposals** designed to slow the Adoption Workflow or force public reconsideration of settled decisions without new information.
- **Misrepresenting credentials**, including claims to be a survivor, a clinician, an advocate, or a legal professional when that claim is material to the participant's influence on decisions.

## Specific provisions for coercive-control content

The coercive-control mini-binder (docs/07-coercive-control/) has elevated sensitivity.

- **Do not open PRs on CC documents without prior Discussion.** Unsolicited PRs on CC content will be closed and the contributor directed to the Expert Review Convening process.
- **Do not include identifying details** of specific cases, parties, or judges in any CC-related Discussion or Issue. Abstract scenarios only.
- **Do not forward CC draft content to broader audiences** (mainstream media, policy forums, legislative staff) prior to expert review.
- **Assume a survivor is reading every thread.** Phrase critiques accordingly.

## Reporting

Report Code of Conduct violations to the maintainer by email (contact TBD) or by filing a private GitHub issue marked `conduct`. Reports are handled privately. Retaliation against reporters is itself a Code of Conduct violation.

## Enforcement

The maintainer has final authority on Code of Conduct enforcement. Responses may include:

- Private notice to the participant.
- Public correction.
- Temporary block from the repository.
- Permanent removal.
- Referral to platform-level conduct enforcement (GitHub).

For methodology governance disputes (as distinct from conduct violations), escalation runs through the Methodology Board and Data Ethics Review, not through the Code of Conduct. The two processes are separate.

## Survivor-specific protections

Survivors of intimate partner violence and coercive control are welcome to participate in this repository. The maintainer will support survivor participation including via:

- Pseudonymous contribution where the contributor's safety requires it.
- Communication routing through advocacy partners where the contributor prefers.
- Removal of contributions at the contributor's request, at any time, for any reason, without requirement to explain.
- Proactive blocking of participants who target a survivor contributor regardless of whether the targeting crosses a formal Code of Conduct threshold.

If you are a survivor considering participation and have safety concerns, contact the maintainer privately before opening any public issue or PR. The maintainer will work with you on a participation mode that keeps you safe.

## Attribution

This Code of Conduct adapts elements from the Contributor Covenant (v2.1, https://www.contributor-covenant.org/) with substantial additions specific to survivor safety and legal-adjacent contexts. The survivor-specific provisions draw on the participation practices of the Missouri Coalition Against Domestic and Sexual Violence and equivalent organizations.

## Changes

Changes to this Code of Conduct follow the Adoption Workflow rather than ordinary PR review. Proposed changes should be filed as Discussions and will be treated with the same governance rigor as methodology changes.
